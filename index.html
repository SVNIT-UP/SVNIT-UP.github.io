Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation

AMRUT stands for Atal Mission for Rejuvenation and Urban Transformation. It is a flagship mission launched by the Government of India in 2015 to improve the urban infrastructure and services in 500 cities across the country. The mission focuses on water supply, sewerage, and solid waste management, among other areas. The primary objective is to improve the quality of life of urban residents and create more livable and sustainable cities. AMRUT aims to achieve this by investing in urban infrastructure and services, and promoting public-private partnerships. AMRUT 2.0: Launched in October 2021, this version focuses on a circular economy of water, including recycling and reusing treated sewage, rejuvenating water bodies, and water conservation.
